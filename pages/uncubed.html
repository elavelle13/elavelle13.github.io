I was employed as an intern at NYC Uncubed from May - August 2015. Through this experience I learned to become a better web designer, understand UX Design concepts, and learn how a small business works.

In May 2015, Uncubed wanted to create a new brand that would make the company stand out. The company wanted to focus more specifically on applying the new brand to their website and email campaigns

My challenge was to create a user interface that would showcase the new brand in an innovative and appealing way. With collective thoughts and ideas on best design practices, I hoped to create a user interface that followed the new brand guidelines in a creative way.

Design and development were broken into parallel workstreams for Uncubed's new website and email templates. I assisted with the design and implementation for all aspects related to For Companies, 404 Error Page, Delivery, and Wakefield (email templates).

I worked page by page, meeting weekly, to report on my progress and discuss any complications with taking the mockups to production. I collaborated with other developers through Github, simultaneously working to provide global patterns and themes when developed throughout the entire process. Pages were tested across multiple platforms to ensure transitions from mobile to desktop were accurate.

The email templates for Wakefield were an ongoing project from May - August 2015. The templates were created through Email on Acid and tested on multiple browsers, phones, and email providers.

Through discussions, design and testing, I curated 3 key elements to establish a user interface that engages users and creates the best possible user expierence:

  • Seamless scroll feature of associated companies

  • Transitions that work for mobile and desktop displays

  • Email template that works on any browser, provider, or phone
