Lunch Money Buddy is a mobile application intended for parents/guardians to manage their kids' in-school cafeteria lunch accounts.

  • The Discovery

I led the research of personas, user journeys and design concepts for Lunch Money Buddy since the outset of the project in June 2017. I took into consideration the customer and business insights to develop a strategy and vision for the user interface.

Starting July 2017, I led the user interface design and execution for Lunch Money Buddy across mobile platforms.

The clients had a general idea of functionalities they wanted to feature on their mobile application, but did not know how to implement it to suit their audience's needs.

My challenge was to create a user interface that was easy to navigate and understand, no matter the user's tech background, while also sticking to the mobile app specifications. With research on the users and the best design practices, I hoped to created a user interface that matched the user's needs and behaviors.

Research, design and prototyping were broken into parallel workstreams. I led the research and design for all aspects related to the user's journey, page structure and best design practices.

I created the mobile app's sitemap and low fidelity wireframes based on the project specifications given. I conducted peer reviews with colleagues to get feedback on my page layouts and concepts. The finalized wireframes were used to create the prototype showcasing the design and functionality of the application.

Through research and design, I curated 4 key elements to establish a user interface that could be understood by a diverse audience:

  • Simple and quick setup process

  • Calendar with similar design concepts

  • Profiles to keep track of each child

  • Ability to ad funds on the go
