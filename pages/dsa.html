The Division of Student Affairs at Kent State University is home to the Vice President's office. Four designers and myself make up the Division's design team, also known as DSA Design.

I am the User Interface Design Specialist & Design Lead for the entire Division. As the Design Lead, I am responsible for hiring and managing the design team members, maintaining and developing the website, and overseeing all of the design projects in the Division. I ensure brand standards are executed at all times while also allowing room for creativity and uniqueness.

In January 2016, the Division realized they needed to update their brand across all digital platforms and printed material. They lacked a website that showcased who they were as a Division, provide useful information and keep users engaged with the site.

My challenge was to create a flawless user interface that met the standards of the University's brand while also providing a greater user experience. With a better online presence we hoped to get useful information to our audience with ease.

Research, design and development were broken into out-of-the-ordinary workstreams for the Division's website. I led the research and design for all aspects related to content types, page structure and design implementation. For this particular project, I worked backwards by starting with design first then research.

In January 2016, mockups were developed based on the University's brand guidelines and standards. After peer reviews and modifications, the mockups were implemented in the development phase of the website in Drupal.

In October 2017, I conducted user research that resulted in the website's new sitemap and wireframes/mockups. This was achieved by conducting online surveys and usability tests to gain insight on the Division's strengths and weaknesses.

Through research and design, I curated 4 key elements to establish a user interface that would help improve the overall website and its user experience:

  • Better grouping for top navigation that shows a clear relationship between each item

  • Shortening content to a maximum number of lines or creating a bullet list

  • New navigation labeling that is understandable to students

  • Highlighting important resources for students on the homepage
