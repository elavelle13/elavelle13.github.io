IdeaBase was approached by Treno, a high-scale Italian restaurant located in Kent, OH, to create their website. The team behind this project consisted of Graphic Designers, Web Developers, and User Experience Designers.

I led the research of competitiors and design concepts for Treno since the outset of the project in April 2017. I took into consideration the customer and business insights to develop a strategy and vision for the user interface.

Starting June 2017, I led the front-end development for Treno across mobile, tablet, and desktop platforms.

In May 2017, the owners of Treno realized they needed an online presence. They lacked a website to showcase who they were as a restaurant, provide useful information and compete with similar restaurants in their area.

Our challenge was to create a seamless user interface that met the needs of the client while also providing a greater user experience. With an online presence we hoped to attract more business for Treno.

Research, design and development were broken into parallel workstreams for Treno. I led the research and design for all aspects related to content types, page structure and design implementation.

I conducted a competitive analysis that would result in the website's sitemap and low fidelity wireframes. I oversaw the designer's high fidelity wireframes that would assist with the front-end development of the website. I worked with the developers to execute the designs through to completion.

Through research and design, we curated 4 key elements to establish a user interface that offered an upscale atmosphere that met the needs of the business and its users:

  • Interactive menu with unique search features

  • Interactive map with unique design features

  • Specials and new menu items highlighted

  • Call for reservations/no online option
