Layover is a mobile application concept to help travelers get to where they need to be.

I led the research on users and their behaviors, brand strategies and best design practices for layover since the outset of the project in March 2016.

Starting October 2017, I revisited the project to begin refining wireframes for prototyping.

In March 2016, the client wanted to create a mobile application that would provide useful information to people traveling in airports. The client wanted to focus more specifically on users who had layovers.

My challenge was to create a user experience that would reflect the behaviors and needs of the client's audience. With research on their target audience and best design practices, I hoped to create a user interface that matched with the user's needs and behaviors.

Research and design were broken into parallel workstreams for Layover. I led the research and design for all aspects related to target audience, color theory, typography, mobile functionalities, and page layouts.

I conducted a competitive analysis that would result in the website's sitemap and low fidelity wireframes that were later used to create my final mockups.

Continuing this project, I will use the mockups to create a live prototype.

Through research and design, I curated 4 key elements to establish a mobile application idea that takes the stress out of traveling:

  • Group traveling made easy

  • Travel readjustment made simple

  • Flight info and reminders stored together

  • Interactive map with unique functionalities
